# Business Impact Rationale

## Operational Value

- Reduces manual dispatcher effort during intake and triage by pre-routing tickets against deterministic rules and AI recommendations
- Improves consistency in queue assignment and SLA application, since both flow from the same data model rather than dispatcher memory
- Speeds up technician handoff with pre-drafted internal context (root cause, next step, escalation guidance)
- Cuts repetitive customer communication work while preserving the review step — reviewers see a word-level diff of what changed before an outbound update is recorded
- Makes sensitive actions visible and governed: procurement, tier 3 escalation, and controlled closure all require an explicit human approval that lands in the audit trail

## Service Leadership Value

- Surfaces SLA risk before breaches become customer escalations, via the dashboard heat map and per-ticket countdowns
- Gives approvers a focused queue for exceptions and higher-risk actions, separated from day-to-day ticket work
- Produces reporting that can support automation investment decisions — the Automation Opportunities page scores recurring categories against the live ticket volume

## Why It Matters For AI Tooling

Most AI demos are chatbots. This one is deliberately shaped around decisions:

- Every AI suggestion is shown alongside the deterministic rule it would replace or reinforce, so reviewers can see when AI and rules disagree
- Divergence between rule-based and AI-recommended routes is surfaced as its own KPI
- Drafts generated by AI never go out without a human review step, and that review is captured as a diff rather than a binary approve/reject
- Audit events distinguish rule-based, AI-assisted, human-approved, and manual sources so trust in the system can be measured over time
